ABSTRACT (12pt., bold)


Climate change is one of the most pressing challenges facing humanity today, with far-reaching consequences for both natural and cultural landscapes. This research paper delves into the intricate relationship between climate change, cultural heritage preservation, and the development of sustainable habitats. It examines the multifaceted impacts of climate change on cultural heritage sites and explores strategies for mitigating these effects while promoting sustainable human habitats. The paper begins by providing a comprehensive overview of climate change and its various manifestations, highlighting the global significance of the issue. It then delves into the vulnerability of cultural heritage, emphasizing the threats posed by rising sea levels, extreme weather events, temperature fluctuations, and other climatic factors. Case studies from around the world are presented to illustrate the tangible risks faced by iconic heritage sites, underscoring the urgency of addressing this issue. The paper proceeds to discuss the interconnectedness of cultural heritage and sustainable habitat development. It explores the role of heritage preservation in fostering a sense of identity and belonging within communities, ultimately contributing to the social and economic well-being of local populations. Additionally, it highlights the potential synergies between heritage conservation and sustainable urban planning, as well as the integration of traditional knowledge systems in climate adaptation strategies. Furthermore, the research investigates various adaptive measures and innovative technologies that can be employed to protect cultural heritage sites from the impacts of climate change. These include structural interventions, climate-resilient design principles, and the application of digital documentation and monitoring tools. The paper also addresses policy and governance frameworks at the local, national, and international levels, emphasizing the need for a holistic and collaborative approach to safeguarding cultural heritage while promoting sustainable habitats. It discusses the role of heritage organizations, governments, and grassroots initiatives in building resilience against climate change effects. In conclusion, this research paper underscores the intricate interplay between climate change, cultural heritage, and sustainable habitats. It advocates for a comprehensive and proactive approach to safeguarding our shared cultural heritage in the face of climate change, recognizing that the preservation of these treasures is not only a cultural imperative but also a crucial component of building resilient and sustainable communities for the future.


Keywords: Climate Change; Cultural Heritage; Sustainable Habitat; Urban Planning; SDG
